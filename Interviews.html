What do you do for work?

  • MYDAD: Works as a machinist for Chevron. Been with them for less than a year but a machinist for over twenty. Typically makes, replaces, or maintains pumps and other machinery.
  • Vic: Works for Applied Medical in the production department, making internal parts for medical devices.
  • Boomshakalaka: Worked as a construction manager for a small company, managing project progress and ensuring efficiency. Also contributed to some construction tasks.

What are your values?

  • MYDAD: Values family above all else and takes pride in his work, striving for quality.
  • Vic: Values faith, family, and works diligently because of their faith, which motivates their work ethic.
  • Boomshakalaka: Values reputation, life, and family, with a strong emphasis on safety at work and respect for others.

Does your work support your values?

  • MYDAD: Yes, managers and colleagues align with shared values of doing quality work and fostering a collaborative environment.
  • Vic: Work supports family through income but does not align with other personal values. Prefers more alignment between work and personal beliefs.
  • Boomshakalaka: Finds fulfillment in ensuring employee safety but feels the salary does not meet expectations.

What is your personality like?

  • MYDAD: Introverted but enjoys socializing during breaks. Prefers working alone to focus but builds good relationships with coworkers.
  • Vic: Friendly, cheerful, and careful. Past personality was more outgoing and open.
  • Boomshakalaka: Introverted and hardworking. Though reserved, becomes more communicative as needed for work tasks.

Are you satisfied with your job?

  • MYDAD: Satisfied with work, environment, and colleagues. Commute and benefits outweigh higher salaries elsewhere.
  • Vic: Dissatisfied due to job not meeting career expectations. Criticizes outdated management techniques.
  • Boomshakalaka: Salary and boss’s carelessness impact satisfaction. Frustrations arise with communication challenges among team members.

What keeps you motivated?

  • MYDAD: Intrinsic motivation to produce quality work. Recognition and awards (R&A) at Chevron provide additional incentives.
  • Vic: Motivated primarily by the need to pay bills, given a lack of alignment with career goals.
  • Boomshakalaka: Family provides the primary motivation. Reminds himself of this to maintain drive and effort.

Do you work in a team?

  • MYDAD: Often works in teams where tasks are distributed and completed individually or collaboratively as needed.
  • Vic: Works in shift teams, collaborating with other leads to support operators and maintain machines.
  • Boomshakalaka: Works in a single team across various sites, focusing on daily task completion and planning with the boss.

Do you work in person, remote, or hybrid?

  • MYDAD: Works entirely in person due to the hands-on nature of his job.
  • Vic: Works in person but would prefer remote work for privacy and isolation.
  • Boomshakalaka: Works in person and likes it but would opt for a hybrid model if available.

Does your mood affect your work?

  • MYDAD: Believes mood can influence work quality but strives to maintain high standards despite emotions.
  • Vic: Job is basic and doesn’t require much attention, so mood doesn’t significantly impact performance.
  • Boomshakalaka: Keeps emotions aside to ensure precision and safety in construction. Changes in mood could affect efficiency but prioritizes professionalism.

Have you experienced stereotypes or discrimination?

  • MYDAD: Workplace is diverse, and he has not experienced significant stereotypes or discrimination.
  • Vic: Observes stereotypes in their department but has not faced discrimination personally.
  • Boomshakalaka: No experiences of discrimination. Team treats each other as equals with mutual respect and camaraderie.
